House Escape

Game Jam ProjectJul 2024

A psychological thriller built in Unity and Blender 4.0, featuring custom lighting, sound, and puzzle systems designed to build tension.

Overview

House Escape is a short psychological thriller built for the WTF x IGDC Game Jam. The game leans on environmental storytelling: custom lighting rigs and layered sound design build tension as the player solves puzzles to escape.

Built in Unity by a five-person team, with custom assets modeled and textured in Blender 4.0 within the jam's time constraints. My contribution was scripting the lighting and sound systems, programming exactly when each light cue and sound trigger fires relative to the player's position and story beats.

Engineering Decisions

  • Scripted lighting timing. Programmed when dynamic light and shadow shifts trigger relative to the player's position, using them to signal danger and guide the player instead of relying on UI cues.
  • Programmed sound trigger timing. Wrote the logic deciding when ambient and reactive audio cues fire as the player approaches key story beats, so tension escalates without relying on jump-scare scripting alone.
